INVENTIONS FACTORY -- Edison's Company

The inventions factory: Edison opened a new laboratory in Menlo Park, NJ, in 1876. This site later become known as an "invention factory," since they worked on several different inventions at any given time there. Edison would conduct numerous experiments to find answers to problems.

How some of the Inventions worked

Incandescent Lightbulb

An incandescent bulb typically consists of a glass enclosure containing a tungsten filament. An electric current passes through the filament, heating it to a temperature that produces light.

Phonograph

Sound is collected by a horn that is attached to a diaphragm. The sound causes vibrations in the air that travel down the horn causing the diaphragm to vibrate. The diaphragm is connected to a stylus and pressed into a cylinder covered in wax (or alternatively a thin layer of tin foil).

Vitascope

uses light to cast images. The images being cast are originally taken by a kinetoscope mechanism onto gelatin film. Using an intermittent mechanism, the film negatives produced up to fifty frames per second. The shutter opens and closes to reveal new images.

Early Childhood

Born in Ohio, USA, 1847, he was the youngest of seven children. However at the age of 12, he had almost lost his entire hearing. He did not let it discourage him and found that it enabled him to concentrate on his work. He also had an early fascination for mechanial things and chemistry

Early Entrepreneuship

In 1859, Thomas A. Edison sold candy and newspapers on board a train in 1859. He set up his own laboratory and printing press to publish his own newspaper on the train. However, a fire ended his experiments. But, he learnt to be a telegraph operator and continued his experiments while travelling around America.

Telegraph Career

Thomas A. Edison formed the American Telegraph Works which developed a telegraph system to send 2 messages simultaneously in both directions. He also received patents for improvements of the telegraph.

Inventing Career

Thomas A. Edison opened the Invention Factory in 1876. In 1877, he invented an improved version of the telephone transmitter. (It was able to send voices louder and clearer than the original version). He also invented the phonograph in the same year which could record and play back sounds. In 1882, he produced the first long-lasting light bulb and an electric light system. In 1912, his company invented the motion picture projector. However, he passed away in 1931 leaving behind 1093 patents to his name.
